Passwords: The key to your identity

Passwords are the only thing that stands in between a hacker and your bank account or social media or even your online identity. So it turns out that those little phrases or codes are far more important than you think it is.

But unfortunately majority of the people do not understand the importance of having a strong password. They find it annoying to have long and complicated passwords for their social media accounts. Instead they prefer short and memorable ones.

A strong password is a combination of all types of characters like uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, punctuations etc. It should also be at least eight characters long. Do not use common information such as date of birth, phone numbers, and names of relatives as passwords as it can be easily hacked by social engineering or a dictionary attack. Using same passwords for multiple accounts will make things even worse. If one of your accounts is hacked, the hacker can instantly access all the other accounts with the same password.

According to a report from September 28, 2018, Facebook announced it got hacked and 50 million accounts were compromised. It also occurs that an average annual revenue from illegal data trading is 160 billion USD.
